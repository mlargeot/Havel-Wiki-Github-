## 1. Overview

The **Havel** platform is designed for cybersecurity professionals, educators, and students who organize or participate in **Capture The Flag (CTF)** events.

## 2. Primary Personas

### **Administrator / Event Organizer**

**Profile:**  
An instructor, cybersecurity club leader, or company representative organizing a CTF competition.

**Goals:**

- Create, configure, and launch CTF events.
- Manage users, teams, and permissions.
- Deploy and monitor challenges in a stable, scalable environment.
- Track performance and resolve incidents during the event.

**Pain Points:**

- Complex manual setup of challenges (Docker, VMs, network).
- Difficulty scaling or customizing events.
- Lack of unified monitoring or centralized management.

**Needs:**

- A **simple and reliable interface** for event management.
- **Automation** of challenge deployment and resource cleanup.
- **Security and isolation** between challenge instances.
- **Performance monitoring** and analytics during events.

**Key Interactions in Havel:**

- Event creation and setup interface.
- Challenge management dashboard.
- User/team management modules.
- Performance and resource monitoring tools.

### **Challenge Creator / Technical Contributor**

**Profile:**
A developer, engineer, or teacher creating technical challenges for CTF competitions.

**Goals:**

- Design challenges involving containers, virtual machines, or network infrastructures.    
- Test and validate challenges before deployment.
- Manage versioning, dependencies, and performance parameters.

**Pain Points:**

- Time-consuming deployment and validation processes.
- Limited support for complex infrastructure-based challenges.
- Lack of documentation or templates for challenge creation.

**Needs:**

- Access to a **challenge SDK** or API for quick deployment.
- Integration with existing tools (Docker Compose, Terraform, etc.).
- Capability to run **local POCs** or sandbox environments.


**Key Interactions in Havel:**

- Challenge editor and metadata form.
- Automated provisioning (Docker/VM).
- Validation and benchmarking modules.

### **Mentor / Technical Expert**

**Profile:**  
A DevOps engineer, security expert, or infrastructure specialist providing feedback and validation for the project or event setup.

**Goals:**

- Review technical architecture and performance metrics.
- Provide insights and validate the deployment strategy.
- Collaborate with the Havel team to improve scalability and security.

**Pain Points:**

- Limited access to technical documentation or monitoring data.
- No centralized way to review performance benchmarks.
- Difficult to validate infrastructure efficiency.

**Needs:**

- Access to **architecture documentation**, logs, and metrics dashboards.
- Clear **performance reports and KPIs**.
- Communication and collaboration tools for providing feedback.

**Key Interactions in Havel:**

- Architecture documentation and dashboards.
- Performance reports.
- Collaboration channels or shared monitoring spaces.

## 3. Secondary Personas

### **Participant / Player**

**Profile:**  
A student or cybersecurity enthusiast taking part in a CTF event hosted on Havel.

**Goals:**

- Access and solve challenges.
- Submit flags and track progress.
- Compete individually or as part of a team.

**Pain Points:**

- Slow challenge startup or network latency.
- Platform instability or challenge crashes.
- Poor visibility on score or event status.

**Needs:**

- **Fast challenge deployment** with minimal latency.
- **Responsive interface** for accessing challenges and submitting flags.
- Reliable feedback and scoring system.

**Key Interactions in Havel:**

- Challenge dashboard and submission system.
- Real-time leaderboard and notifications.
- Secure communication channels.


### **Infrastructure / DevOps Operator**

**Profile:**  
A system administrator or DevOps engineer managing the hosting infrastructure of Havel.

**Goals:**

- Deploy and maintain the Havel environment.
- Ensure platform uptime and resource scaling.
- Monitor performance and fix issues quickly.

**Pain Points:**

- Heavy workloads during events.
- Manual monitoring or limited observability tools.
- Resource bottlenecks under high traffic.

**Needs:**

- **Automated deployment pipelines** (Docker Compose, Kubernetes).
- **Monitoring dashboards** for CPU, memory, and network usage.
- **Alerts and logging systems** integrated into the backend.

**Key Interactions in Havel:**

- Infrastructure deployment scripts (Docker, Compose, K8s).
- Monitoring systems (Grafana, Prometheus).
- Backend performance logs and alerts.
